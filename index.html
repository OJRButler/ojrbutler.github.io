About Me

I'm Oliver Butler, a final-year Mechatronics Engineering student at the University of Canterbury and a core contributor to UC Motorsport, our Formula SAE electric racing team.

I’ve always been drawn to the technical side of motorsport not just how things work, but how to make them better, faster, and more reliable. Working on EV systems, embedded firmware, and control strategies in a competitive team has been a huge part of that. I enjoy building things that actually get used and tested under pressure.

This site showcases some of the projects I’ve worked on over the past few years both independently, apart of UC Motorsport, and throughout my degree.

Projects

Below are some of the key projects I have completed both through UC Motorsport, personal interests, and my degree.

UC Motorsport Electrical Team

Current Sense PCB

Designed and fabricated a high-speed current sense PCB with CAN output and LEM sensor, used in real-time current monitoring for EV telemetry.

Pedalbox PCB

Designed a safety-critical brake threshold detection PCB using STM32 and CAN communication, deployed in Formula SAE racecar brake system.

Centre Lock Hub Adapters

Designed custom centre-lock-to-4-stud adapters to interface a racecar spindle with a Dynapack hub dynamometer for torque validation testing.

Personal Projects

3D Printer Covid Face Masks for Health Workers

Was apart of the ShieldsUp initiative where we Designed, 3D printed and delivered custom face shields for health workers during the Covid 19 pandemic

NA MX5 Car Restoration

Restored a classic NA Mazda MX-5, handling all prep work, priming and painting myself. This used many hands-on and technical engineering skills.
